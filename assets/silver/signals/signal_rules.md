# Silver signal rules

## Primary rule groups

### Bullish silver
Bullish when most of the following are true:
- Real yields are falling
- USD is flat or weakening
- Gold is supportive
- Silver is holding or reclaiming important support
- Industrial demand is improving or at least not deteriorating
- ETF flows are supportive

### Bearish silver
Bearish when most of the following are true:
- Real yields are rising
- USD is strengthening
- Gold is weak
- Silver is losing or failing to reclaim important support
- Industrial demand is weakening
- Policy expectations are moving tighter-for-longer

### Neutral silver
Neutral when:
- bullish and bearish drivers conflict,
- timestamps are too mixed,
- or price action has not confirmed the macro and industrial view.

## Cross-market modifiers

### Gold / silver ratio modifier
- A falling gold/silver ratio strengthens the bullish silver case.
- A rising gold/silver ratio weakens the bullish silver case and can indicate relative underperformance.

### Industrial-demand modifier
- Strong manufacturing, solar, or fabrication demand strengthens the silver case.
- Weak industrial data can offset a supportive precious-metals backdrop.

### Safe-haven modifier
- Silver does not always behave like a clean haven.
- If geopolitical stress rises but silver fails to respond while gold leads, downgrade the safe-haven bullish effect.

## Decision mapping

### Strong bullish
Use only when:
- real yields are falling,
- USD is not a headwind,
- gold is supportive,
- price confirms strength,
- and industrial demand is not weakening.

Action:
- Hold or add if risk rules allow.

### Mild bullish
Use when:
- the macro picture is improving,
- but confirmation is incomplete.

Action:
- Hold, watch for confirmation.

### Neutral
Use when:
- macro, industrial, and precious-metals signals conflict,
- or the signal quality is degraded by mixed timestamps or unconfirmed price behavior.

Action:
- Hold or stay flat.

### Mild bearish
Use when:
- real yields and USD are a headwind,
- price is weak,
- or silver is underperforming gold,
- but industrial or sentiment support prevents a stronger bearish signal.

Action:
- Reduce or stay flat.

### Strong bearish
Use when:
- real yields are rising,
- USD is strengthening,
- gold is weak,
- price is breaking support,
- and industrial demand is not offsetting macro pressure.

Action:
- Exit, avoid adding, or stay flat.

## Required analyst checks
Before assigning a signal, answer:
1. What are real yields doing?
2. What is the dollar doing?
3. Is gold supportive or weak?
4. What is the gold/silver ratio doing?
5. Is industrial demand improving or deteriorating?
6. Are ETF flows reinforcing or only cushioning the move?

## Default behavior
If the answer is mixed, uncertain, or poorly timestamped:
- default to Neutral
- reduce confidence
- avoid forcing a trade
