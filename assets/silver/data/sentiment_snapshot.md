## Raw facts
- Major geopolitical developments: AP reported on `2026-03-26` that Iran has formalized a de facto "toll booth" regime in the Strait of Hormuz, with shipping traffic through the strait down sharply since the war began.
- Manufacturing or industrial news: ISM reported a February 2026 manufacturing PMI of `52.4`, showing continued expansion but slower than January.
- Solar or electronics demand themes: Reuters reported on `2026-02-19` that solar manufacturers are accelerating substitution away from silver toward copper as silver prices stay elevated.
- ETF flow context: iShares reported `SLV` ounces in trust at `499,592,395.30` on `2026-03-11` and `498,776,192.20` on `2026-03-25`, a small decline rather than a fresh accumulation surge.
- Precious-metals sentiment: the Silver Institute said on `2026-02-10` that investment demand should remain strong in 2026 even as industrial fabrication declines.
- Major news themes affecting silver: the active themes are geopolitical stress, tighter-for-longer macro, persistent silver-market deficit language, and concern that high prices are causing solar-demand substitution.

## Interpretation
- Silver is trading like a mixed metal rather than a clean haven. Geopolitical stress is high, but that has not translated into an obviously strong silver tape.
- Industrial demand is mixed. Manufacturing is still expanding, but solar substitution is a real drag on one of silver's most important demand channels.
- ETF demand looks more like a cushion than a fresh upside accelerator because official trust ounces slipped modestly over the last two weeks of available data.
- The dominant theme is mixed: geopolitical support and structural deficit language are competing with tighter-for-longer macro and industrial-demand substitution risk.

## Uncertainty
- Geopolitical news is current and fast-moving. The transmission into silver can change quickly if markets start treating silver more like a monetary metal again.
- ETF and demand data are slower-moving than same-day headlines. They are useful context, but not clean same-day tactical drivers.
- The industrial-demand story is directionally clear but not uniform: stronger manufacturing and weaker solar intensity can both be true at the same time.
