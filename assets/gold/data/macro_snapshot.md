# Macro snapshot

## Timestamp
- Updated: 2026-03-20 10:35 CET

## Raw facts
- US 10Y nominal yield (`DGS10`): 4.26% on 2026-03-18.
- US 10Y real yield (`DFII10`): 1.86% on 2026-03-18.
- US 10Y breakeven inflation (`T10YIE`): 2.36% on 2026-03-11.
- Broad USD index (`DTWEXBGS`, broad trade-weighted dollar, not DXY): 119.491 on 2026-03-06.
- FOMC decision on 2026-03-18: target range held at 3.50% to 3.75%.
- The FOMC stated that inflation remains somewhat elevated and that the implications of Middle East developments for the U.S. economy are uncertain.

## Interpretation
- Real yields remain elevated near 1.9%, which is a macro headwind for gold.
- The broad dollar data available in accessible public sources is stale versus the rate data. It showed a firmer dollar into early March, but I cannot verify a newer broad-dollar reading from the same source set here.
- The Fed held rates steady on 2026-03-18 rather than delivering fresh easing. That is not gold-friendly at the margin.
- Breakeven inflation is still above 2%, but not high enough on its own to offset the pressure from elevated real yields.

## Uncertainty
- The repo asks for `DXY`, but the clean accessible public series I could verify here was the Fed/FRED broad dollar index, not ICE DXY.
- Inflation expectations and dollar data are older than the March 18 rate data, so this macro snapshot has mixed timestamps.
