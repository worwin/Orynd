## Interpretation
- Real yields are hurting gold. A 10Y real yield near 1.9% is still a meaningful opportunity-cost headwind.
- The dollar is also not helping gold. The best verified dollar proxy available here is still elevated, even though it is not the repo-preferred `DXY`.
- The Fed backdrop is steady to restrictive, not easing. The March 18, 2026 hold and the shift toward fewer cuts both lean against gold at the margin.
- The energy complex is clearly raising inflation risk through oil and shipping disruption. That can support gold through inflation-shock and geopolitical channels, but it can also hurt gold if it pushes yields and the dollar higher or delays Fed easing.
- The macro regime looks mixed with an inflation-shock bias: energy and geopolitics push inflation risk up, while markets also have to consider slower growth and policy repricing.

## Uncertainty
- `DTWEXBGS` is a broad trade-weighted dollar index, not `DXY`.
- The matched nominal / real / breakeven set above is from 2026-03-11, while the FOMC and energy-market inputs are newer.
- The market-implied easing line above comes from accessible public coverage of futures pricing, not a directly extracted live CME FedWatch probability table.
