# Sentiment snapshot

## Timestamp
- Updated: 2026-03-20
- Time zone: CET

## Raw facts
- Major geopolitical developments: AP reported further escalation in the Iran-Israel conflict on 2026-03-20, including attacks across the Gulf and additional strikes on regional energy infrastructure.
- Oil / shipping / Strait disruption developments: AP reported that the Strait of Hormuz is effectively restricted, with selective passage still occurring; about 90 ships crossed between March 1 and March 15 even as broader disruption risk stayed high.
- Sanctions or export disruption: I could not verify a major same-day new sanctions package from the accessible source set. The cleaner verified disruption channel is physical: attacks on energy assets, chokepoint risk, and selective shipping rather than a fresh sanctions regime.
- Market fear or risk sentiment: MarketWatch summarized Barclays as saying markets are pricing a 50% probability of a severe supply shock, up from 25% the week before.
- Central bank gold demand context: World Gold Council said central banks bought 863.3 tonnes in 2025, with Poland the largest buyer at 102 tonnes.
- ETF flow context: World Gold Council said February 2026 was the ninth straight month of global gold ETF inflows, with holdings up 26 tonnes to 4,171 tonnes and global flows of US$5.3 billion.
- Major news themes affecting gold: geopolitical stress is very high, but gold is not acting like a clean haven because higher yields, dollar firmness, and delayed Fed-cut expectations are offsetting part of the safe-haven bid.

## Interpretation
- Geopolitical stress is rising, not falling.
- The stress is likely to affect inflation through energy and shipping first. That is the clearest transmission channel into gold right now.
- Gold is not behaving like a clean haven. The market is also trading the inflation, rates, and dollar consequences of the conflict.
- ETF flows and central-bank demand are reinforcing the broader constructive backdrop for gold, but those data are slower-moving than the current news cycle.
- The dominant theme is mixed: safe-haven demand is present, but it is competing with an inflation shock and a tighter-for-longer macro response.

## Uncertainty
- The geopolitical news flow is fast-moving and could change materially after this timestamp.
- ETF-flow and central-bank data are useful context, but they are slower-moving than the same-day conflict and market-repricing headlines.
