# Gold signal rules

## Primary rule groups

### Bullish gold
Bullish when most of the following are true:
- Real yields are falling
- USD is flat or weakening
- Geopolitical stress is elevated
- Gold is holding or reclaiming important support
- ETF flows and/or central-bank demand are supportive

### Bearish gold
Bearish when most of the following are true:
- Real yields are rising
- USD is strengthening
- Gold is losing or failing to reclaim important support
- Geopolitical stress is not producing effective safe-haven demand
- Policy expectations are moving tighter-for-longer

### Neutral gold
Neutral when:
- bullish and bearish drivers conflict,
- timestamps are too mixed,
- or price action has not confirmed the macro view.

## Cross-market modifiers

### Oil / energy shock modifier
- Oil rising is not automatically bullish gold.
- Treat oil as bullish for gold only if it raises inflation and instability without causing an even stronger rise in real yields and the dollar.
- Treat oil as bearish or neutral for gold if the oil shock mainly strengthens the dollar, lifts real yields, or hardens policy expectations.

### Geopolitical transmission modifier
- Geopolitical stress strengthens the bullish case only when gold is actually attracting safe-haven demand.
- If war headlines rise but gold falls because yields and USD rise faster, downgrade the geopolitical bullish effect.

### Liquidation / panic modifier
- In sharp liquidation events, gold may sell off even if the medium-term thesis is supportive.
- Do not confuse short-term liquidation with a full structural breakdown unless macro and price both confirm.

## Decision mapping

### Strong bullish
Use only when:
- real yields are falling,
- USD is not a headwind,
- price confirms strength,
- and at least one supportive demand or geopolitical factor is reinforcing.

Action:
- Hold or add if risk rules allow.

### Mild bullish
Use when:
- the macro picture is improving,
- but confirmation is incomplete.

Action:
- Hold, watch for confirmation.

### Neutral
Use when:
- macro and sentiment conflict,
- or the signal quality is degraded by mixed timestamps or unconfirmed price behavior.

Action:
- Hold or stay flat.

### Mild bearish
Use when:
- real yields and USD are a headwind,
- and price is weak,
- but geopolitical support prevents a stronger bearish signal.

Action:
- Reduce or stay flat.

### Strong bearish
Use when:
- real yields are rising,
- USD is strengthening,
- price is breaking support,
- and geopolitical support is not offsetting macro pressure.

Action:
- Exit, avoid adding, or stay flat.

## Required analyst checks
Before assigning a signal, answer:
1. What are real yields doing?
2. What is the dollar doing?
3. Is gold above or below key support?
4. Is geopolitical stress producing actual safe-haven demand?
5. Is oil creating inflation support for gold or tighter-for-longer pressure against it?
6. Are ETF flows and central-bank demand reinforcing or only cushioning the move?

## Default behavior
If the answer is mixed, uncertain, or poorly timestamped:
- default to Neutral
- reduce confidence
- avoid forcing a trade
