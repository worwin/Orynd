CHAPTER 20 PER-SHARE EARNINGS: How WARREN TELLS THE WINNERS FROM THE LOSERS er-share earnings are the net earnings Of the company on a per-share basis for the time period in question. This is a big number in the world of investing because, as a rule, the more a company earns per share the higher its stock price is. To determine the company's per-share earnings we take the amount of net income the company earned and divide it by the number of shares it has outstanding. As an example: If a com- Pany had net earnings of SIO million for the year, and it has one million shares outstanding, it would have per-share earn- ings for the year of SIO a share. While no one yearly per-share figure can be used to iden- tify a company with a durable competitive advantage, a per- share earnings figure for a ten-year period can give us a very clear picture of whether the company has a long-term compet- itive advantage working in its favor.
