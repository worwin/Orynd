Also, in the United States, in determining whether or not the personal holding company tax applies, treasury shares are not part of the pool of the outstanding shares, when it comes to determining control of the company. Unscrupulous types will represent to the IRS that they only own 49% of all out- standing shares. But if they subtract the treasury shares, as the law says they should, they really control in excess of 50%, which gives them control of the business and potentially makes them liable to the personal holding company tax.
