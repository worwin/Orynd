# C09 - Cost of Goods Sold For Warren the Lower the Better

## Purpose
Lower and steadier cost of goods sold leaves more room for durable gross profit.

## Source
- PDF: `C09 - Cost of Goods Sold For Warren the Lower the Better.pdf`
- OCR text: `C09 - Cost of Goods Sold For Warren the Lower the Better.txt`

## Buy Signals
- Prefer businesses with a low and stable cost-of-goods-sold ratio
- Look for cost structure stability over time
- Treat efficiency as supportive when it is durable

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid businesses where cost of goods sold eats most of revenue
- Avoid large swings caused by competition
- Avoid thin-margin commodity setups unless other evidence is exceptional

## AI Rules
- If cost_of_goods_sold_ratio is low and stable THEN add positive evidence
- If the ratio rises persistently THEN reduce moat confidence
- Use this as an input into gross-margin quality, not as a standalone trigger
