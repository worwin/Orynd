# Valuation Masterclass

Scope: Chapters `C53` through `C57`

## Goal
Translate business quality into an action: buy, watch, hold, or sell.

## Required Output
- `valuation_status`: `Buy`, `Watch`, `Hold`, or `Sell`
- `starting_yield_view`
- `yield_on_cost_growth_view`
- `timing_context`
- `sell_trigger_check`
- `valuation_notes`

## Rule Set
- Treat a durable business like an equity bond where pretax earnings are the coupon.
  Source: `C53`
- Starting price matters because it determines the initial yield on those earnings.
  Lower price means higher long-run return.
  Source: `C53`, `C56`
- Prefer businesses whose earnings can grow and therefore increase yield on cost over time.
  Source: `C54`, `C55`
- The best entry windows are usually bear markets or one-time solvable problems.
  Source: `C56`
- Do not force a buy at euphoric prices even if the business is excellent.
  Source: `C56`
- Default action for a wonderful business is to hold while the moat remains intact.
  Source: `C57`
- Consider selling when the moat is eroding, when capital can move into a clearly better opportunity, or when valuation becomes extreme.
  Source: `C57`
- A rough extreme-overvaluation warning from the book is a P/E around `40x` or more on one of these super businesses.
  Source: `C57`

## Decision Guide
- `Buy`: high-quality business plus attractive price, bear market entry, or temporary solvable problem.
- `Watch`: high-quality business, but the price is not yet attractive enough.
- `Hold`: moat remains intact and valuation is not extreme.
- `Sell`: moat deterioration, clearly better redeployment opportunity, or extreme overvaluation.
