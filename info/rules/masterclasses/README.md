# Buffett Masterclasses

These rulebooks consolidate the 57 chapter readmes into the five book sections, but they now preserve concrete thresholds, lookbacks, exceptions, and chapter traceability.
Use these section rulebooks when reviewing a company statement-by-statement.

## Working Order
1. `introduction/README.md`
2. `income_statement/README.md`
3. `balance_sheet/README.md`
4. `cash_flow_statement/README.md`
5. `valuation/README.md`

## Rulebook Standard
- Each section keeps explicit thresholds when the book gives them.
- Each section keeps explicit lookbacks when the book gives them.
- Each rule points back to the chapter IDs that support it.
- If the book treats an item as contextual rather than decisive, the rulebook says so explicitly.

## Suggested Company Workflow
For a company workspace such as `assets/equities/tickers/AAPL/`, a clean Buffett-style flow is:
- `candidate_screen.md`
- `income_statement_review.md`
- `balance_sheet_review.md`
- `cash_flow_review.md`
- `valuation_review.md`
- `final_buffett_evaluation.md`

## Section Outputs
- Introduction: decide whether the business is even a Buffett-style candidate.
- Income Statement: rate business economics and earnings quality.
- Balance Sheet: rate resilience, leverage, and capital structure quality.
- Cash Flow Statement: rate cash conversion and reinvestment burden.
- Valuation: decide buy, watch, hold, or sell.

## Rating Idea
Use section ratings such as `Strong`, `Mixed`, and `Weak`, then combine them in the final report.
Valuation should be expressed as an action state such as `Buy`, `Watch`, `Hold`, or `Sell`.
