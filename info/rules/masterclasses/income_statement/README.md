# Income Statement Masterclass

Scope: Chapters `C07` through `C20`

## Goal
Judge business economics and earnings quality from the income statement.

## Required Output
- `income_statement_rating`: `Strong`, `Mixed`, or `Weak`
- `revenue_view`
- `gross_margin_view`
- `operating_expense_view`
- `interest_burden_view`
- `pretax_income_view`
- `tax_quality_view`
- `net_earnings_view`
- `per_share_earnings_view`
- `normalized_earnings_notes`

## Metric Rules
- Revenue should be stable or rising and ideally tied to repeat demand rather than one-off volume surges.
  Source: `C08`
- Lower and steadier cost of goods sold is better because it leaves room for durable gross profit.
  Source: `C09`
- Gross margin is an early moat test.
  `40%+` over a long period is positive.
  `50% to 60%+` is especially strong when consistent.
  `20% or below` is a warning sign for fierce competition.
  Lookback: roughly `10 years`.
  Source: `C10`
- SGA should be controlled relative to gross profit.
  `Under 30%` of gross profit is excellent when consistent.
  `Near or above 100%` repeatedly is a major danger sign.
  Source: `C12`
- Heavy ongoing R&D is a durability problem in this framework because it often means the business must constantly reinvent itself.
  Source: `C13`
- Depreciation is a real economic cost and should not be ignored with EBITDA-style shortcuts.
  Source: `C14`
- Interest expense should be low.
  In ordinary operating businesses, `below 15%` of operating income is strong.
  Within one industry, the lowest interest burden is usually better.
  Source: `C15`
- One-time gains and losses should be removed from normalized earnings.
  Source: `C16`
- Pretax income should be stable and rising because it is the base for later valuation work.
  Source: `C17`
- Taxes paid should broadly make sense relative to pretax income.
  Source: `C18`
- Net earnings should show a long-run upward trend.
  Source: `C19`
- Per-share earnings should also rise over time, but EPS must be checked against real net-income growth so buybacks do not create a false win.
  Source: `C19`, `C20`

## Hard Negatives
- Gross margin `20% or below` without a compelling offsetting explanation.
- SGA repeatedly near or above `100%` of gross profit.
- Heavy structural R&D dependence with no clear durability.
- Interest burden that consumes a large share of operating income.
- Pretax income, net earnings, or EPS that are flat, erratic, or declining.
- Reported profit driven by one-time gains.

## Rating Guide
- `Strong`: gross margin `40%+`, manageable expenses, low interest burden, and durable upward trends in pretax income, net earnings, and EPS.
- `Mixed`: some good economics are present, but one or two major areas still need caution.
- `Weak`: thin margins, high expense drag, heavy interest burden, or poor earnings quality.
