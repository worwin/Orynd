# C48 - Return on Shareholders' Equity Part Two

## Purpose
High returns on shareholders' equity usually mean the company is putting retained earnings to work effectively, which Buffett loves.

- PDF: `C48 - Return on Shareholders' Equity Part Two.pdf`

## Buy Signals
- Prefer businesses with higher-than-average and sustained ROE
- Treat 20% to 30%+ ROE as strong evidence when it is durable and not debt-driven
- Allow unusual negative-equity cases only when earnings history is clearly strong and the cause is buybacks

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid persistently low ROE businesses
- Avoid companies whose high ROE comes mainly from leverage or equity shrinkage
- Avoid negative-equity companies with weak earnings histories

## AI Rules
- IF ROE is sustainably high and leverage-adjusted THEN add strong positive evidence
- IF ROE is low THEN classify the business as lower quality
- IF equity is negative THEN distinguish between buyback-driven strength and real impairment before deciding
