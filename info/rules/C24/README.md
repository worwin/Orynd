# C24 - Cash and Cash Equivalents Warren's Pile of Loot

## Purpose
A large and durable cash pile can be evidence of a strong moat, but Buffett distinguishes operating cash generation from one-time financing or asset sales.

## Buy Signals
- Prefer lots of cash and marketable securities when debt is low
- Review the past seven years of balance sheets to confirm cash came from operations
- Treat cash strength as especially valuable in temporary trouble

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid companies with weak cash positions and heavy debt
- Avoid treating a one-time cash windfall as proof of a moat
- Avoid cash-rich companies whose cash came mainly from financing

## AI Rules
- IF cash rises over a seven-year review period while debt stays low and earnings stay strong THEN add positive evidence
- IF cash came from financing or asset sales THEN classify the signal as weak
- Use cash versus debt as a survival test in troubled situations
