# Master Rulebook Scaffold

This is the first consolidation layer built from the 57 chapter readmes.
It is intentionally high level so we can refine, deduplicate, and score the rules later.

## Foundations
- Confirm the business has a durable competitive advantage.
- Confirm the business model is understandable and repeatable.
- Do not proceed to valuation if quality is not established first.

## Income Statement Rules
- Prefer consistent revenue, gross profit, pretax income, net earnings, and per-share earnings.
- Prefer high gross margins, manageable operating costs, low interest burden, and limited R&D dependence.
- Normalize one-time gains and losses out of recurring earning power.

## Balance Sheet Rules
- Prefer large cash piles created by operations, not financing.
- Prefer little short-term and long-term debt.
- Prefer low treasury-share-adjusted debt-to-equity and strong retained earnings growth.
- Prefer buyback evidence and high-quality ROE that is not leverage-driven.

## Cash Flow, Valuation, and Timing Rules
- Prefer strong operating cash flow and low capital-expenditure needs.
- Prefer buybacks funded by excess cash rather than debt.
- Value the business through durable pretax earnings and growth in yield on cost.
- Buy in bear markets or on one-time solvable problems. Consider selling only on moat erosion, a clearly better opportunity, or extreme overvaluation.
