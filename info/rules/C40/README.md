# C40 - Long-Term Debt Something That Great Companies Don't Have a Lot Of

## Purpose
One of Buffett's favorite signs of a great business is little or no long-term debt because the company can fund itself from internal economics.

## Buy Signals
- Prefer businesses with little or no long-term debt over a long window
- Treat it as excellent when annual earnings could repay long-term debt within about three to four years
- Use low debt as evidence that the business does not need outside capital

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid debt-heavy companies that need borrowing to stay competitive
- Avoid judging only the current year, study a decade
- Avoid businesses whose debt would take many years of earnings to repay

## AI Rules
- IF long_term_debt_payback_years <= 4 THEN score positively
- IF the business shows many years of low long-term debt THEN treat that as strong moat evidence
- IF long-term debt is persistently high THEN classify the business as lower quality
