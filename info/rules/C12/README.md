# C12 - Selling, General, and Administrative Expenses

## Purpose
SGA is a useful moat test because durable businesses often keep it comparatively low and stable versus gross profit.

## Source
- PDF: `C12 - Selling, General, and Administrative Expenses.pdf`
- OCR text: `C12 - Selling, General, and Administrative Expenses.txt`

## Buy Signals
- Treat SGA below 30% of gross profit as excellent when consistent
- Allow some higher levels if the pattern is stable and other metrics are strong
- Focus on multi-year consistency

## No-Buy Signals
- Be skeptical when SGA nears or exceeds 100% of gross profit
- Avoid businesses with wild SGA swings
- Do not treat low SGA as enough if R&D, capex, or interest remain heavy

## AI Rules
- IF sga_to_gross_profit < 30% AND stable THEN score positively
- IF sga_to_gross_profit is near or above 100% repeatedly THEN classify economics as weak
- Use SGA with R&D, capex, and debt checks before deciding
