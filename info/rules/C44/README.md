# C44 - Preferred and Common Stock Additional Paid in Capital

## Purpose
Businesses with durable competitive advantages often do not need expensive preferred stock or repeated equity issuance.

## Buy Signals
- Prefer companies with no preferred stock and limited need to issue new equity
- Treat self-financing as a positive moat signal
- Use capital stock history to judge whether growth comes from strength or fundraising

## No-Buy Signals
- Be cautious when a company relies on preferred stock
- Avoid repeated share issuance that dilutes owners
- Avoid businesses that cannot grow without tapping equity markets

## AI Rules
- IF preferred_stock is absent and share issuance is limited THEN add positive financing-quality evidence
- IF the business repeatedly raises equity to survive or grow THEN reduce quality
- Use capital-stock history to distinguish internally funded compounding from dependence on outside investors
