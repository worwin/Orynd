# C42 - Total Liabilities and the Debt to Shareholders' Equity Ratio

## Purpose
A low treasury-share-adjusted debt-to-equity ratio is a useful Buffett filter, especially outside financials.

## Source
- PDF: `C42 - Total Liabilities and the Debt to Shareholders' Equity Ratio.pdf`
- OCR text: `C42 - Total Liabilities and the Debt to Shareholders' Equity Ratio.txt`

## Buy Signals
- Prefer adjusted debt-to-equity ratios below about 0.80 in non-financial businesses
- Adjust for treasury stock before drawing conclusions
- Treat low liabilities relative to equity as evidence of internal financing strength

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid very high adjusted debt-to-equity ratios outside financials
- Do not use the unadjusted ratio blindly when buybacks shrink equity
- Avoid comparing banks to industrial companies without context

## AI Rules
- IF adjusted_debt_to_equity < 0.80 and the business is not a financial institution THEN add positive evidence
- IF the ratio is very high THEN downgrade quality
- Always adjust for treasury shares before evaluating buyback-heavy companies
