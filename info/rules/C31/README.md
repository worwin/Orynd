# C31 - Intangible Assets Measuring the Unmeasurable

## Purpose
Reported intangible assets are not a reliable moat detector on their own, because internally developed brands and franchises often do not appear on the balance sheet at all.

## Buy Signals
- Treat reported intangibles as supporting context, not as the primary signal
- Favor businesses whose brand or franchise strength shows up through long-term earning power
- Recognize that strong internally developed brand assets may be economically real even when the balance sheet does not show them

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid paying up for accounting intangibles without durable earnings and cash generation
- Avoid relying on reported balance-sheet labels instead of business economics
- Avoid assuming weak reported intangibles means the business has no brand-based moat

## AI Rules
- Treat reported intangible assets as secondary evidence and verify them through long-term margins and earnings
- IF reported intangibles are low or absent THEN do not assume the company lacks a brand moat
- IF brand or franchise strength is economically obvious but not recorded on the balance sheet THEN rely on long-run earning power rather than the intangible-asset line
