# C30 - Goodwill

## Purpose
Goodwill mainly tells Buffett that a company has been buying businesses above book value, by itself it is neither good nor bad.

## Source
- PDF: `C30 - Goodwill.pdf`
- OCR text: `C30 - Goodwill.txt`

## Buy Signals
- Treat rising goodwill as acceptable when acquisitions are sensible and economically strong
- Recognize that excellent businesses rarely sell below book
- Use goodwill as a clue about capital allocation

## No-Buy Signals
- Avoid treating goodwill itself as proof of quality
- Avoid acquisitive strategies without a record of buying strong businesses
- Avoid assuming all acquisition-driven growth is value-creating

## AI Rules
- Classify goodwill as acquisition evidence, not moat evidence
- IF goodwill rises THEN inspect whether acquired businesses improved returns
- IF a great business ever trades below book THEN flag it as a possible special opportunity
